# Research And Psychology Notes

## Core Positioning Logic

- Homeowners need quick trust, not technical explanations.
- Local service sites convert better when the first screen answers three questions immediately:
  - what do you do
  - where do you serve
  - how do I contact you right now

## Why This Direction Was Chosen

- Tree service is high-trust and high-consequence.
- Storm work, removals, and difficult-access jobs demand confidence signals.
- Real field photos and real equipment proof are more persuasive than generic polish by itself.

## Psychology Used

- Authority through geography:
  county-based map plus city pins creates regional credibility fast.
- Safety through clarity:
  emergency work and storm readiness are easier to believe when shown visually and simply.
- Trust through specificity:
  55 ft reach, family ownership, free estimates, and county coverage are concrete.
- Premium perception through motion:
  layered depth and restrained 3D create memorability without looking noisy.
- Conversion through reduced friction:
  direct call path, estimate block, and visible offers keep the next step obvious.

## Reusable Lesson

The best local-business redesigns do not invent authority. They uncover the authority the business already has and present it with sharper hierarchy, better pacing, and stronger visual trust.
